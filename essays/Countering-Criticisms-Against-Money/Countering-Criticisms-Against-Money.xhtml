Countering Criticisms Against Money

The aim of this essay is to counter two criticisms I encountered against the concept of money.

Buy Nothing Day

In “Buy Nothing Day” people are encouraged to pass a day without buying anything using money, but instead exchange “gifts” using barter. The problem with that is that the invention of money, which is generally agreed to be convenient, predates more recent and more controversial trends such as capitalism, commercialism, and consumerism, and its use should not be discouraged. As someone wrote to me when I mentioned it to him: “Money allows us to translate dissimilar items into a way in which they can be compared and traded. Going back to ‘my Perl code for 100 bales of hay’ is a recipe for disaster.”.

The economy is not perfect, and economists do not agree of the proper way to fix it, but abolishing money would be a huge step backward.
