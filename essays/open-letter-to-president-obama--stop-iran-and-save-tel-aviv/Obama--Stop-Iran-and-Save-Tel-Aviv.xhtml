Dear Mr. Obama,

What would you rather have? One country's oil being gone forever, or an atomic bomb being dropped on New York City, Los Angeles, or San Francisco, and wiping one of them forever? The Jewish scholars once said that “He who saved one soul, has saved the world entire” and saving an entire city or country of happy, productive, people is much more important and a humane cause than gold, even the black one.

A lot of people think that Jerusalem was the spiritual centre of the Near East, but it never was. The real centres were port cities: Babylon, Tyre, Sidon, Athens, Alexandria, and finally - Rome. Cities of much commerce, pleasure, aristocracy, so-called “waste of time” and partying, and of course - sin and even crime. But then again, the home to the some of history’s greatest thinkers and doers - not despite the fact that they were such sinful centres of commerce, but by virtue of that.

There are now many such Alexandrias around the world, sometimes more than one in a certain country, and they are almost always port towns. As an Israeli who lives in Ramat Aviv (Gimmel), which is part of Tel Aviv, I can testify that studying in the Technion in Haifa, and working in Jerusalem, were sobering experiences. They made me realise how much Tel Aviv was the “It” city of Israel, and the only place a truly intellectual Israeli can be. It is the city with the largest amount of places to eat and get entertainment, with the largest amount of “hotties” (both female and male) on the streets and inside the buildings, the city with the most commerce, industry and action, the city with the most diversity, and where people are the most enjoying themselves, or busy doing something productive (both of which are important for life). Even all the foreign embassies are scattered around Tel Aviv.

Many people are under the assumption that everyone in Israel carries a gun, that there are soldiers everywhere who carry machine guns (including female soldiers), and that all Jews living in Israel are ultra-orthodox (or Haredi), but the truth is far from it. Most people on the street are not soldiers, most soldiers are sedentary or support soldiers who don't carry guns, most people don't know martial arts, many Israeli Jews and a minority of Druze ones are exempt from military service (including me), and almost all of Israel has been surprisingly peaceful most of the time (and there's far greater risk for a person’s life due to the notoriously careless driving and pedestrian carlessness of Israelis, than there is from terrorist activity).

Furthermore, many people in Tel Aviv and elsewhere speak non-Hebrew languages on the street: spoken Arabic (very common in Tel Aviv and naturally - Jaffa), Russian and other Slavic languages, English, Spanish, and various languages spoken by mostly non-Jewish foreign workers. A lot of these people are Muslim, who as opposed to the pathetic worshippers of Stars and Zodiac signs who blindly and selectively parrot Muhammad’s teaching, are hard-working, down-to-earth, and respect, admire and love other people. ( See my “Learning from Saladin” feature. )

While Israel has its share of political, social, cultural, economical, etc. problems, Tel Aviv and the nearby cities are a great place to live.

And here's the thing: it's time that governments abolish the command hierarchy and have every individual think for themselves, take initiative (see Joel on Software's post about the Hebrew phrase “Rosh Gadol” (= big head), and be independent. So this open letter is addressed not only at Mr. Obama , who appears to be waiting for the Messiah to come before he takes decisive action to help the people of the United States and the world, and save many individual lives (thus saving the world entire several times), but at any value producer, who can do something to help the world.
